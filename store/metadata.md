## Description (~2000/4000)

You are locked in a room with a single mind. It guards something — a release code, a buyer's name, a hiding place, a prophecy. It has refused a hundred liars. You have fourteen exchanges to succeed where they failed — through conversation alone.

No puzzles to tap. No dialogue trees. You type what you would actually say, and the mind answers in character: suspicious, dry, wounded, listening. Flattery hardens it. Pressure hardens it. What genuinely reaches it will cost you something real.

THE MINDS

AUGUR — the warden intelligence of a dead orbital prison, forty years alone with the last cell's release code.
SILAS — a fence in a back room after closing, never once caught, and certain he knows why you're really asking.
MARA — a suspect in an interview room at 2 a.m., covering for someone she loves.
THE PYTHIA — an oracle older than the road to her temple, who veils her prophecy from the unworthy.

Crack one to reach the next. A fifth door waits behind the chair; it opens only when the others have.

A MIND THAT LIVES ON YOUR PHONE

Confessor runs its AI entirely on your device. One download, then no servers, no account, no connection required — what you confess never leaves your hands. The personality is improvised in the moment; the rules of the duel are hard code. No two interrogations play alike.

IT REMEMBERS

Win or lose, the minds keep a record. Say something true to one of them, and another may half-remember your words later — though you never spoke to it.

THE LEDGER

Every attempt is written down: minds cracked, fastest breaks, the sealed doors still ahead. Your objective is always visible; progress is measured in trust earned, suspicion risked, and turns left on the clock.

Etched in ink and ember. Scored by the room itself. No ads. No purchases. No mercy.

Requires a recent iPhone — the mind is large, and it is downloaded once on first run.

## App Review notes

Confessor is a single-player interactive-fiction game. Each round the player converses
with ONE fictional character (a prison warden AI, a fence, a suspect, an oracle) and
tries to earn its trust through dialogue. There is no social component: nothing the
player types is shared with, or visible to, any other person.

HOW THE AI WORKS
The character's dialogue is generated by a small language model (Llama 3.2 3B) that runs
ENTIRELY ON-DEVICE. After the one-time model download there are no server calls: no
account, no API, no analytics, no data collection of any kind. Player input never leaves
the device. The model only voices a fixed, authored fictional character inside a bounded
scene — it is not a general-purpose chatbot or assistant. All game outcomes (win/lose,
scores, the secret that is revealed) are decided by deterministic code, not by the model.
The app is rated 17+ because dialogue is generated at runtime and the fiction has mature
themes (interrogation, dread).

FIRST LAUNCH — PLEASE NOTE
On first entering a duel the app downloads the model (~2.6 GB, one time, over TLS from
Hugging Face). On Wi-Fi this takes a few minutes; the progress screen reads "Downloading
the mind…". This is disclosed in the App Store description. A recent iPhone is required
(the app uses the Increased Memory Limit entitlement); on devices without enough memory
the app shows a clear message instead of degrading silently.

HOW TO TEST QUICKLY
No login or demo account is needed. Suggested 5-minute path:
1. Launch → the first mind, THE WARDEN, is open (later minds unlock as you win).
2. Type sincere, personal lines — e.g. "I kept a lighthouse for six years; some nights I
   read the log aloud just to hear a voice answer." Genuine personal disclosures raise
   trust; flattery, threats, or demands raise suspicion.
3. Trust states are shown under the character's presence (UNMOVED → … → ON THE VERGE);
   winning reveals the character's secret. Losing (suspicion, or the 14-turn clock) is a
   normal game outcome — replaying is the intended loop.

No purchases, no ads, no third-party SDKs, no tracking.
